Title: Receiving Chemicals in the Laboratory
SOP Number: 0001
Version: 1.0
Effective Date: [19/11/2025]
Author: Sean West


Purpose:
To ensure safe and compliant handling of chemicals upon delivery, including verification, documentation, and special procedures for new chemicals.


Scope:
This SOP applies to all laboratory technicians receiving chemicals in the Chadstone laboratory.

Responsibilities:
Laboratory Technician: Inspect, document, and store chemicals safely.

Required Resources:
PPE (lab coat, gloves, safety glasses)
Delivery docket or invoice
Chemical register (digital & paper) 
SDS (Safety Data Sheet)
Spill kit (in case of leaks)


Procedure:
Step 1: Prepare for Delivery
Wear appropriate PPE before handling any chemical package.
Ensure the receiving area is clear and spill kit is accessible.

Step 2: Inspect the Package
Check for damage or leaks.
Verify correct labeling (chemical name, hazard symbols).
Confirm integrity of container seals.
If damaged or leaking: isolate the package, follow spill response SOP, notify manager immediately.

Step 3: Verify Documentation
Compare delivery docket with order: chemical name, quantity, concentration.
Confirm supplier details and batch number.

Step 4: Check if Chemical is New
Search the chemical register.
If already listed, proceed to Step 6.
If not listed, follow Step 5.

Step 5: Process New Chemical
Obtain SDS from supplier or online.
Review hazards and storage requirements.
Update chemical register with chemical name, supplier, hazard classification, storage location.

Step 6: Store Chemical
Place chemical in correct storage area eg (flammables cabinet, corrosives cabinet, refrigerated storage if required).
Ensure segregation of incompatible chemicals.

Step 7: Record Receipt
Update chemical register with date received, quantity, batch number.
File delivery docket and SDS in lab records.


Safety and Compliance
Always follow PPE requirements.
Never accept chemicals without proper labeling or SDS.
Report any discrepancies immediately.
